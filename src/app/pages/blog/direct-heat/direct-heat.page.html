Direct Heating

Heating your home can be overlooked until your heating system fails or your electricity bill increases rapidly. Heating systems become major problems if they are not working properly. While it is important to provide adequate heat in your homes, keeping costs down is also critical. If you are looking for a new heating system or would like to learn more about direct heat, this article is for you. We will discuss the advantages and disadvantages of various types of direct heating systems to help you determine which may be right for you.

Direct heat comes usually in the form of a space heater and acts as a supplementary heat source. Some homes, however, use direct heating systems for their entire house. This type of heating system is very easy to install, but it is more expensive to operate than a central heating system.

What Is a Direct Heater?

Direct heaters heat air that passes through an open flame, and components in these systems control the warmed air. Direct heating systems are effective in areas with less insulation or large amounts of warm air.

There are many advantages to direct heating systems:

  1. They are very efficient as 100% of the combustion gases are used to heat the air.
  2. Some of these systems can operate in temperatures as low as -30 degrees Fahrenheit.
  3. Direct heaters are less expensive to purchase, rent, and maintain.
  4. Simple components make up these heaters making them very easy to transport.
  5. Even small direct heaters produce high amounts of heat, and many have built-in thermostats.

Some disadvantages of direct heaters include:

  1. An inability to be used in tightly sealed spaces or near flammable items
  2. Heating spaces must have a type of window, door, or other air exchange to open.
  3. In some cases, these systems do not always create dry heat.
  4. Exhaust and fumes produced are some of the combustion byproducts.

Gas Space Heaters Explained

Natural gas and propane space heaters are mostly used in garages, basements, and outdoor spaces converted into rooms. Systems like wall-mounted, free-standing, and floor furnaces have a lack of ductwork and do not produce very much heat. Due to a lack of ducts, gas space heaters are most useful in heating a single room. To heat multiple rooms with this type of system, doors must be left open between rooms. The most advanced systems use sealed combustion air systems with installed pipes to provide combustion air and carry off combustion products. Gas space heaters are most appropriate for buildings like cabins in which large temperature differences between rooms are acceptable. Most models use natural gas or propane while some burn kerosene.

Some advantages to these systems include:

  1. Natural gas and propane are cheaper than electricity so these heaters are more economical.
  2. These space heaters provide immediate heat and come equipped with a thermostat to maintain a constant temperature.

Disadvantages include:

  1. Systems must be vented to the outside to avoid carbon monoxide leaks.
  2. Installation is more complicated than with other direct heating systems.
  3. Gas-fueled space heaters also lack cool-touch grilles like electric space heaters have, so children and pets must be kept away.

Unvented gas-fired space heaters have many health and safety risks. These systems are only safe if a nearby window is kept open but this defeats the purpose of the heating system itself. The safety risks include exposure to combustion by-products and oxygen depletion. Many states prohibit the use of these heaters in homes, and other U.S. cities have banned them as well.

Electric Space Heaters Explained

Portable electric space heaters may be inexpensive to buy but they are costly to utilize. Electric current is converted from the wall socket directly into heat. It takes much more electricity than natural gas or oil to deliver the same amount of useful heat. Electric resistance heat is generally the most expensive form of heat and rarely recommended. However, these systems are readily available in stores and only require a power outlet.

Electric baseboard heat is another type of resistive heating, and it is hard-wired. Its installation cost is low and individual thermostats can be installed in each room to easily turn the heat down. Unless your home is extremely insulated, the operating costs for these systems are very high.

Some advantages of electric space heaters include:

  1. They come in various styles such as fan, ceramic, and radiator-style heaters.
  2. Regardless of how much money you have to spend, there will be an electric space heater that fits into your budget.
  3. Electric space heaters have safety features like cool-touch grilles and tip sensors that will shut off the system if it is tipped over.

Disadvantages of electric space heaters include:

  1. They use a great deal of power which will increase your electricity bill.
  2. The oil-filled radiator style heaters are not very portable and tend to be more expensive than the smaller electric space heaters.

Wood and Pellet Stoves Explained

In rural areas, wood and pellet heating may make sense. Stacking wood and stoking the stove or furnace would be necessary tasks with this type of heating. Wood prices are usually lower than oil, gas, or electricity. Therefore, if you cut your own, savings can be high. However, there are regulations governing pollution emissions from wood stoves as wood pollutants have become a major problem in the U.S. Consequently, new models of stoves are clean-burning. Pellet stoves are less polluting than wood stoves and give users greater convenience, temperature control, and indoor air quality.

Wood stove technology has come a long way and EPA-certified stoves are efficient and burn cleanly. This results in making far less of an impact on the environment.

Some advantages of wood and pellet stoves include:

  1. The fuel being inexpensive and renewable

  3. There are very eco-friendly stoves with catalytic converters for the most environmentally friendly option.
  4. Pellet stoves offer more options than wood stoves and are generally more eco-friendly as well.

Some disadvantages to these types of stoves are:

  1. They must be vented to the outdoors, so installation may involve construction which is time consuming.
  2. You may need a dry, covered spot to store fuel for the stove.
  3. If wood is the source of fuel, significant storage space may be needed to use the wood stove daily.

Fireplaces Explained

Gas and wood fireplaces are usually a part of home decor. However, they are not usually an effective heating system. They rely on air drawn from the room into the fireplace for combustion and dilution. This results in a greater heat loss than heat gain as so much warm air is drawn through the unit and has to be replaced by colder air outside. If the fireplace has a tight-sealing glass door, an outside air source, and good chimney damper, it can provide effective heat to a home.
